Travelling near the sky

Curated ideas for experiencing Peru with only a backpack. Walk through Inca stones at dawn, chase salt-lake sunsets, and sleep under the brightest southern stars.

Start in Cusco Find an adventure

Cusco Historic

Base yourself at altitude (3,399 m) to acclimatize and wander from colonial plazas to hidden courtyards.

Must do

Sunrise at Sacsayhuamán

  • Climb early to watch alpenglow on Andean peaks.
  • Pack coca tea; mornings are crisp even in dry season.
Half day

San Blas craft walk

  • Stone streets, ateliers, and airy cafés for a slower pace.
  • Pick up handwoven textiles direct from artisans.
Evening

Plaza de Armas after dusk

  • Catch folk music under colonnades with warm street light.
  • Try aji de gallina or rocoto relleno from tiny kitchens.

Transports

Move light: combine buses for budget legs and trains for scenic windows.

Bus

Cusco → Puno

  • Night bus with 160° seats; choose lower deck for smoother ride.
  • Arrive for floating islands on Lake Titicaca at dawn.
Train

Ollantaytambo → Aguas Calientes

  • Vistadome cars give the best river-and-jungle frames.
  • Book the earliest slot to beat mid-morning crowds at Machu Picchu.
Shared taxis

Valle Sagrado hops

  • Collectivos between Pisac, Urubamba, and Chinchero run all day.
  • Carry coins; fares are fastest with exact change.

Adventure Activities

Choose altitude-friendly outings first, then push higher once your body agrees.

Day trek

Laguna Humantay

  • Emerald glacier lake at 4,200 m; start before 6:00 to avoid clouds.
  • Layers and rain shell essential — weather flips fast.
Multi‑day

Salkantay trek

  • Less crowded approach to Machu Picchu with high passes and jungle finish.
  • Plan 4–5 days; book mules locally for lighter packs.
Adrenaline

Colca Canyon

  • Descend twice the depth of the Grand Canyon; spot condors on thermals.
  • Hydrate — Arequipa heat sneaks up after chilly mornings.

Accommodation

Stay small-scale to meet other backpackers and locals.

Cusco

San Blas hostels

  • Terraced views over tiled roofs; short walk to Plaza.
  • Ask for rooms away from the street for quieter nights.
Andes

Homestays

  • Lake Titicaca islands and Colca villages offer family stays.
  • Dinners are simple, hearty, and perfect after long hikes.
Jungle

Eco-lodges

  • Puerto Maldonado lodges bundle river transport and guided walks.
  • Pack biodegradable repellent; humidity is relentless.
Ready to go lighter? Save the map offline, carry layers, and keep sunrise slots for the magic hours.
